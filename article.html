Popular Attractions

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a symbol of freedom and democracy that stands on Liberty Island in New York Harbor. It was a gift from the people of France to the United States and was dedicated in 1886.

Visitors can take a ferry to Liberty Island to see the statue up close, and also visit the nearby Ellis Island National Museum of Immigration.

The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials, generally built along an east-to-west line across the historical northern borders of China to protect against raids and invasions.

Today, visitors can hike along various sections of the wall, and also visit nearby attractions such as the Ming Tombs and the Forbidden City.

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is an Incan citadel set high in the Andes Mountains in Peru, renowned for its dry-stone walls that fuse huge blocks without the use of mortar, intriguing buildings that play on astronomical alignments and panoramic views. Its exact former use remains a mystery to archaeologists and visitors alike.

Visitors can take a train or hike the Inca Trail to reach Machu Picchu, and also explore the nearby Sacred Valley and Cusco, the former capital of the Inca Empire.
